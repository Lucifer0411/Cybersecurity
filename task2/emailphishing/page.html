Task 2: Analyze a Phishing Email Sample.
  • Objective: Identify phishing characteristics in a suspicious email sample.
  •  Tools: Email client or saved email file (text), free online header analyzer.
  • Deliverables: A report listing phishing indicators found

In this task take some smaples of samples of phishing emails.

1. Microsoft Password Change



Following are the indicators phishing email
  • The email address support@msupdate.net is not a legitimate Microsoft support email and is likely a phishing attempt.
  • Check for real emails: Legitimate emails from Microsoft will have a sender domain of @microsoft.com or @accountprotection.microsoft.com.

Clicking on "Reset your password" can lead you to the security compromise.

2. Software Update Phishing Email



Signs of a software update phishing email
  • Generic greeting: The email uses a generic greeting like "Dear User" instead of your name. 
  •  Sense of urgency: It creates a sense of panic by saying your account will be suspended or blocked if you don't act immediately. 
  •  Suspicious links: Links might look legitimate but lead to a different, malicious URL when you hover over them. Be wary of links that ask you to log in to verify your account.
